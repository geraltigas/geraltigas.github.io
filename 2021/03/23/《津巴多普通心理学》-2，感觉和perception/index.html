《津巴多普通心理学》-2，感觉和perception

definition:
sensation: the process by which the receptor creates a pattern of neural messages that present the stimulus in the brain
perception: a mental process that elaborates and assigns meaning to the incoming sensory patterns

their relation: perception reinterpret the sensation giving sensation meanings

Core concept 2.1:
the brain senses the world inderitly by receving the neural messages converted from stimulation by sense organs

the info flow:
stimulation -> transduction -> sensation -> perception

the factors determining whether and how the stimulation is converted into senation:

  1. transductions
  2. sensory adaptation
  3. thresholds(value)
    1. fuzzy boundary
    2. weber’s laws(the same change makes less difference when the intensity is higher)
    3. the signal detection thery: sensation depends on
      • the charactristic of the stimulus
      • background stimulation
      • detector
        tips:
  4. It may seem incredible that basic sensations, such as the redness and flavor of our tomato—or the colors Jonathan could see before his accident—are entirely creations of the sense organs and brain.
  5. The whole process (transduction) seems so immediate and direct that it fools us into assuming that the sensation of redness is characteristic of a tomato or the sensation of cold is a characteristic of ice cream. But they are not!
  6. We should consider our senses to be change
    detectors. But because they accommodate to unchanging
    stimulation, we become less and less aware of constant
    stimulation.

core concept 3.2
The senses all operate in much the same way, but each extracts different information and sends it to its own specialized processing region in the brain.

the manifestation of the sensory difference between humans:
to the extent that the sense receptors exhibit some
variation from one person to another, so does our sensory experience of the world. This variability is not so bizarre as to make one person’s sensation of sweet the same as another person’s sensation of sour. Rather, the variations observed involve simply the intensity of taste sensations, such as the bitter detected by supertasters

pain:
pain can arise from intense stimulation of various kinds, such as a very loud sound, extremely bright light, or heavy pressure. however, the pain is not merely the result of the intensive stimulation but is from the moods and expectations.
*nociceptors:*a kind of cell which exist throughout our body recevie the stimulations , convert it into neural signal and send it to central nervous system.

core concept3.3:
Perception brings meaning to sensation, so perception produces an interpretation of the world, not a perfect representation of it.

**percept:**is not just a sensation but also the related meaning

perceptual processing:
the primary visual cortex,at the back of the brain,splits visual infomation into two interconnected streams.
one to
the what pathway: mainly determine what the the objects are.
another to
*the where pathway:*mainlt dtermine the objects location.
curious phenomenon about this two pathway:
a curious phenomenon known as blindsight, a condition that occurs in some people with damage to the what pathway—damage that makes them visually unaware of objects around them. Yet if the where pathway is in- tact, blindsight patients may be able to step over objects in their path or reach out and touch objects that they claim not to see.

after flowing throught the previous two pathway.more specialied processing will be conducted and specific features like length slant color boundary etc will be extracted out.

processing patterns: top-down processing and bottom-up processing

perceptual ambiguity and distortion:we can only feel illusion
