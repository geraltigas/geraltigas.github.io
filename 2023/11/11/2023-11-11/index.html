    Coq

    Posted on 2023-11-11 In exploration

    Purpose

    In logic field, humans cannot compete with computers, and there must be tools to assist humans in logic exploration. Learning Coq is an attempt to do so. The system established with the help of powerful computer tools will be more robust and maintainable.

    My current needs are: I often propose many propositions, but I don’t know how to formalize them and describe them in formal language. At the same time, I need tools to help me prove the propositions I propose. These propositions are not limited to mathematical propositions and include other types of propositions.

    Possible Solutions

    The following responses are from GPT-4.

    Based on your needs, you may require two types of resources and tools:

    1. Learn Formal Methods: First, you need to learn how to formalize propositions, i.e., how to accurately describe your propositions using the language of logic and mathematics. This usually involves knowledge of mathematical logic, set theory, type theory, and other related fields.
    2. Choose the Right Proof Assistant or Tools: Select a tool that can handle various types of propositions. Coq, Isabelle/HOL, and Lean are all good choices because they can handle a wide range of mathematical and logical propositions, including those beyond pure mathematics.

    Here are some suggestions for these two aspects:

    Learn Formal Methods

    • Mathematical Logic Courses: Consider taking online courses or university courses related to mathematical logic and set theory. These courses typically cover propositional logic, first-order logic, proof theory, and foundational concepts.
    • Read Relevant Books: There are many introductory books that can teach you how to do mathematical proofs and logical reasoning, such as “How to Prove It: A Structured Approach,” among others.

    Choose the Right Proof Assistant

    • Coq: It’s well-suited for scenarios that require strong typing and inductive proofs. Coq has a vibrant community and plenty of educational resources and documentation.
    • Isabelle/HOL: If you lean more towards automation and ease of use, Isabelle is a good choice. It has strong automation capabilities compared to Coq and a more modern user interface.
    • Lean: Lean is an emerging proof assistant known for its performance and modern features. If your propositions involve complex mathematical concepts, Lean may be a good choice.

    Practice and Community Engagement

    • Hands-on Practice: Try using these tools to formalize and prove some simple propositions. Practical experience is essential for understanding how to transform propositions into formal language.
    • Join the Community: Participate in relevant online forums and communities, such as the Coq, Isabelle, or Lean tags on Stack Overflow or the official mailing lists for these tools. Communities are great places to seek help and learn new techniques.

    Finally, because formalizing propositions and using proof assistants have a learning curve, please be patient as you learn and practice. With experience, you will become more proficient in using these tools to express and prove your propositions.

    Personal Note

    GPT-4’s breadth of knowledge is impressive, and it can clearly understand and respond to user requests, providing high-density information. It’s well-suited as a guide to understanding new knowledge. Users can start from their own needs, and GPT-4 can provide very practical learning paths.

    Further Plans

    • Start learning Lean, with a focus on building the formal system I need from the most basic Lean syntax and on proof automation.
    • Learn how to formalize propositions.

    目的

    在逻辑上,人是比不过计算机的,肯定有工具帮助人进行逻辑上的探索,学习Coq是一次尝试. 强大计算机工具帮助下建立的体系将会更加坚固,可维护.

    我目前的需求是：我经常会提出很多命题，但是我不知道怎么形式化，以及用形式化的语言描述出来。同时，我还需要工具帮助我证明我提出的命题。这些命题不只是数学命题，包括其他的命题类型。

    可能的解决办法

    以下回答来自GPT4

    根据您的需求，您可能需要两方面的资源和工具：

    1. 学习形式化方法：首先，需要学习如何将命题形式化，即如何用逻辑和数学的语言准确地描述您的命题。这通常涉及数理逻辑、集合论、类型理论等领域的知识。
    2. 选择合适的证明助手或工具：选择一个能够处理多种类型命题的工具。Coq、Isabelle/HOL 和 Lean 都是不错的选择，因为它们都可以处理广泛的数学和逻辑命题，包括那些超出纯数学范围的命题。

    针对这两方面，以下是一些建议：

    学习形式化方法

    • 数理逻辑课程：考虑参加有关数理逻辑和集合论的在线课程或大学课程。这些课程通常覆盖命题逻辑、一阶逻辑、证明理论等基础概念。
    • 阅读相关书籍：有许多入门级的书籍可以教你如何进行数学证明和逻辑推理，例如《How to Prove It: A Structured Approach》等。

    选择合适的证明助手

    • Coq: 非常适合于那些需要强类型和归纳证明的场景。Coq 的社区也非常活跃，有大量的教学资源和文档。
    • Isabelle/HOL: 如果您更偏向于自动化和易用性，Isabelle是一个不错的选择。它的自动化能力强于 Coq，并且有一个更现代的用户界面。
    • Lean: Lean 是一个新兴的证明助手，以其性能和现代化的特性而闻名。如果您的命题涉及复杂的数学概念，Lean 可能是一个好的选择。

    实践和社区参与

    • 动手实践: 尝试使用这些工具来形式化和证明一些简单的命题。这种实践经验对于理解如何将命题转化为形式化语言非常重要。
    • 加入社区: 参与相关的在线论坛和社区，如Stack Overflow上的Coq、Isabelle或Lean标签，或者这些工具的官方邮件列表。社区是获取帮助和学习新技巧的好地方。

    最后，由于形式化命题和使用证明助手需要一定的学习曲线，因此请耐心学习并实践。随着经验的积累，您将更加熟练地使用这些工具来表达和证明您的命题。

    本人的屁话

    GPT4的知识广度令人惊讶,同时GPT4还能明确听懂用户的请求,回答信息密度高. 很适合作为了解新知识的引路人. 并且用户可以完全从需求出发,GPT4能提供非常切实际的实践路线.

    进一步计划

    • 开始学习Lean, 重点关注 如何从最基础的Lean语法构建出我所需的形式化系统, 以及证明自动化
    • 学习如何将命题形式化
